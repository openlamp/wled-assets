> **Why only 8 named colours?** Naming hundreds of colours is pointless: on a saturated stage LED, most would be indistinguishable and no one remembers 300 names. Instead we take the set of colours the eye can actually tell apart at a glance — **Kelly's colours of maximum contrast** (Kelly 1965), minus the achromatics, in Kelly's *prefix-optimal* order (the first N are the N most distinguishable). **5** is colour-blind-safe; **8** is the sweet spot (it overlaps Wong 2011's palette); beyond ~7–12, at-a-glance discrimination degrades (Healey 1996) — more so on a bright physical LED. So: 8 short, memorable, unmistakable names — not a wall of near-identical ones.
